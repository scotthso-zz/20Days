What if we all followed our passion?

by Scott So

Imagine a world where there was no reward for what you do.

You don't get a raise from a promotion.
You don't get a bonus for working overtime.
You just don't get anything at the end of the day.


Would you still be doing what you're doing now?

You wouldn't.
During this day and age, we are so blinded by money and societal success that we never stop and think, "Am I happy with what I'm doing?". Do you go to work in the morning excited and happy, not because of the free food, massages, insurance and dry cleaning?

If I asked you why you wanted to be a doctor what would you say?
Oh I just want to help those who are sick.

I call bullshit. If doctors made below the average annual salary would you still be saying the same thing?

Of course a lot of you do truly believe in "helping others."

People hear about all these high-paying jobs, "Doctors, Programmers, Investment Banker etc..." and they immediately want to pursue it.

However, I am guilty of this myself. The only reason why I went into Software Engineering over all the other programs is the thought of working for high-profile companies such as Google or Facebook. Never once did I consider, do I actually enjoy learning this stuff. I mean this is my future career we're talking about here and as soon as my offer came in, I didn't hesitate to click the accept button.

One time I was with a good friend of mine and we were talking about our dream home when we get rich. Out of the blue this guy was like
Ya I just want a room where I mount beautiful code on the walls.
To which I was like what?
Ya, just elegant code. A room of code.
Thats when it hit me that I didn't love programming. I liked it, but I wasn't that passionate about it. This guy was.

Now if everyone in the world did what they were most passionate about, imagine how much better our world would be.
